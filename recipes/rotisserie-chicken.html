Rotisserie Chicken

Description

Rotisserie chicken is a cooking classic that combines simplicity with exceptional flavor. It is a whole chicken seasoned with a blend of herbs and spices, such as rosemary, thyme, garlic and paprika, which is slowly cooked in the oven until the skin is crispy and golden brown, while the meat remains juicy and tender. The key to a perfectly roasted chicken is to make sure the bird is well seasoned and cooked to the right temperature, allowing the juices to redistribute during the cooking process.

Accompanied by vegetables such as potatoes, carrots and onions, which are cooked along with the chicken, this dish becomes a complete and comforting meal. The combination of browned meat and caramelized vegetables creates a delicious and satisfying culinary experience, ideal for family dinners or special gatherings. Served with a sauce made from the cooking juices, rotisserie chicken offers a deep, satisfying flavor that everyone will love.

Ingredients

  • 1 (3 pounds) whole chicken
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • vegetables (as potatoes, carrots and onions)
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon ground paprika
  • 1/4 tablespoon ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  2. Season chicken cavity with a pinch of salt. Tie legs together with kitchen string; then tie wings to the bird. Secure chicken on a rotisserie attachment. Add the vegetables inside as well.
  3. Place rotisserie over the preheated grill and cook for 10 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, quickly mix together butter, 1 tablespoon of salt, paprika, and pepper. Turn the grill down to medium and baste chicken with butter mixture.
  5. Close the lid and cook over medium heat, basting occasionally, until chicken is cooked through and the internal temperature reaches 180 degrees F (83 degrees C), 1 to 1 1/2 hours.
  6. Remove chicken from the rotisserie and let rest for 10 to 15 minutes before carving.
